Apptricity CEO Timothy D. Garcia to Address MODEX 2014

Company’s founder, president and CEO is set to speak at the world’s leading supply chain show, March 17-20 in Atlanta.

Apptricity, a leading provider of mobile enterprise software solutions, announced today that CEO Timothy D. Garcia will address the MODEX 2014 show next month in a presentation on supply chain efficiency.

Garcia's presentation, titled “Adapting Your Business to the Age of Now by Improving Supply Chain Management Efficiency,” will be held from 3:45 p.m. to 4:30 p.m. Eastern time in Theater B of the Georgia World Congress Center in Atlanta.

MODEX 2014, sponsored by the Material Handling Industry of America (MHIA), is the newest expo for the manufacturing and supply chain industries. It's where the best and brightest supply chain thinkers gather to discuss the trends and challenges.

About 800 supply chain software providers will be showcased, offering attendees a chance to review and compare various solutions.

Garcia's presentation will cover the following key points in improving supply chain management efficiency:

  • How transformative new tools/technology empowers companies with the efficiency they need to be more competitive.
  • How machine-to-machine technology helps to draw companies closer to customers and their needs.
  • How the latest technology delivers real-time command visibility to distributed businesses up and down the supply chain – from front office to the front lines – via mobile platforms, from iOS to Windows and Android.
  • How 360-degree visibility enables companies to run tighter supply chains with less risk of disappointing customers.

MODEX 2014 features 150 sessions led by industry leaders, including keynotes from Edward H. Bastian, president of Delta Air Lines; former Wal-Mart CEO Lee Scott; and Scott Sopher, principal with Deloitte Consulting.

For more information on MODEX 2014 or to register to attend the show, please visit http://www.modexshow.com/.

About MHIA

MHIA is an international trade association that has represented the material handling and logistics industry since 1945. MHIA members include material handling and logistics equipment and systems manufacturers, integrators, consultants, publishers, and third-party logistics providers. Member companies come from all areas of material handling and various parts of the world, making MHIA a strong national and international representative for the material handling and logistics industry. Much of the work of the industry is done within its product-specific Industry Groups. The association sponsors trade events, such as ProMat and MODEX to showcase the products and services of the industry and to educate manufacturing, distribution and supply chain professionals on the productivity solutions provided through material handling, logistics and related activity.
